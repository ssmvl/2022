Sungshin Women's University — Informed Consent

Study Title:  Relationship between ensemble perception of facial identities and emotional expressions

Principal Investigator:  Oakyoon Cha, Department of Psychology, Sungshin Women’s University, Seoul, Republic of Korea

Investigator:  Soo Jin Kim, Department of Psychology, Sungshin Women’s University, Seoul, Republic of Korea

The purpose of this study is to investigate the relationship between individual’s perceptual abilities of recognizing face identity and facial expression in a crowd of faces. This study is conducted by researchers in Republic of Korea.

Before agreeing to participate in this research study, please read the following carefully to understand the procedures, potential risks, and benefits of the study. Your participation is voluntary and you may discontinue participation at any time without penalty.

Background and Purpose.  Please refer to this document.

Participant.  At least 75 participants (up to 300 participants) over the age of 18 will be recruited.

Procedures.  You will first be asked to provide information about yourself (sex, age, race), and then asked to complete following tasks:

  • Viewing nine faces with various identities, emotions or nine random shapes for less than a second and choose the most frequent identity, emotion, or shape.

Duration.  This study consists of one experiment that requires 30-100 min.

Risks.  Participation involves no more than minimal risk.

Compensation.  Upon satisfactory completion of the study, you will receive the amount listed either in the HIT directory (Amazon Mechanical Turk, MTurk) or in the study directory (Prolific), via the payment system provided by Amazon or Prolific, respectively.

Benefits.  Indirect benefits to participation are contribution to knowledge.

Confidentiality.  The only identifying information gathered is either Worker ID (MTurk) or Prolific ID (Prolific). Both types of ID are an arbitrary string of alphanumeric characters that is not linked to any personal information.

Questions or Concerns.  You can ask questions about this study now or at any time during the study by e-mailing Oakyoon Cha at oakyoon.cha@sungshin.ac.kr

If you have questions about your rights as a research participant or feel that you have not been treated fairly, please contact the Institutional Review Board of Sungshin Women's University at irb@sungshin.ac.kr or at +82 2-920-7760
