Relationship between Multitasking Ability and Ensemble Perception Ability — Background and Purpose of the Study

Background.  Multitasking refers to a human behavior pattern in which one performs two or more tasks simultaneously. Previous studies measured participants’ multitasking experiences using a self-report questionnaire called Media Use Questionnaire (Ophir, Nass, & Wagner, 2009). One characteristic of digital devices is that they provide a large amount of information instantly and facilitate multitasking of the provided information (Hwang, et al., 2014). In this multitasking situation, attention would be distributed over a large amount of information. Ensemble perception is the ability to summarize a large amount of information provided (Baek & Chong, 2020; Chong & Treisman, 2003). To summarize a large amount of information into statistical values, ensemble perception relies on distributed attention (Chong & Treisman, 2003; Alvarez, 2011; Baek & Chong, 2020). Since humans have limited attentional resources, each task would be given little attentional resources during multitasking. Ensemble processing has similarities in that it relies on distributed attention over multiple objects rather than focused attention on each object. This study attempts to find out whether these two processing share the same mechanism.

Purpose.  In this study, we intend to find out the relationship between an individual’s practical multitasking skills and ensemble perception ability.
