Influence of stimulus location on ensemble judgment — Background and Purpose of the Study

Background.  Ensemble perception refers to people’s ability to see a large number of objects and estimate statistical properties quickly (e.g., average size of circles, average orientation of lines, average facial expression). Recently, Bronfman et al. (2014) found that people can judge ensemble properties of objects presented outside the locus of visual attention (i.e., task-irrelevant stimuli). Subsequent studies used the same experimental task to investigate whether people can perceive ensemble properties (i.e., statistical properties of a group of all stimuli) without attention (Jackson-Nielsen et al., 2017; Ward et al., 2016). However, these studies did not check whether participants paid attention to the task-irrelevant stimuli. If some attention was given to task-irrelevant stimuli, this is likely to have influenced the judgment of statistical properties.

Purpose.  In this study, we intend to investigate whether the distribution of attention influence judgment about statistical properties of a group.

In this experiment, you will view an array of letters, and then asked to report a letter that was presented at a given location (memory task). Then, you will judge the diversity of a previously presented array (diversity judgment). We will analyze whether the diversity judgments differ depending on the distribution of attention.

References.

Bronfman, Z. Z., Brezis, N., Jacobson, H., & Usher, M. (2014). We see more than we can report: “Cost Free” Color phenomenality outside focal attention. Psychological Science, 25(7), 1394-1403.
Jackson-Nielsen, M., Cohen, M. A., & Pitts, M. A. (2017). Perception of ensemble statistics requires attention. Consciousness and Cognition, 48, 149-160.
Ward, E. J., Bear, A., & Scholl, B. J. (2016). Can you perceive ensembles without perceiving individuals? The role of statistical perception in determining whether awareness overflows access. Cognition, 152, 78-86.
